# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

ai-tracker is maintained as a public good for everyone building with AI. We want this project to be approachable for first-time contributors, useful for working engineers and researchers, and welcoming regardless of background, experience level, or affiliation.

## Standards

Examples of behavior that builds the kind of community we want:

- **Cite sources.** Authoritative URLs over folklore. Disagreement about a model's specs gets resolved by linking the docs.
- **Be clear, not clever.** Issue and PR descriptions that lead with what changed and why beat ones that assume context.
- **Lower the cost of contribution.** Reviewers explain *why* feedback applies, not just *what* to change. Good first-issue triage and template improvements count as substantive work.
- **Disagree on the artifact, not the person.** Critique schemas, queries, copy, and code — not character.

Examples of behavior we don't want:

- Personal attacks, harassment, dismissive or demeaning comments.
- Public or private harassment of any contributor.
- Publishing others' private information (physical address, email) without explicit permission.
- Knowingly submitting false sources, mis-attributed claims, or AI-generated submissions without disclosure.
- Coordinated brigading or vote-stuffing in the /upvote flow.

## Scope

This applies in all project spaces — the repo, issues, pull requests, the `/submit` queue, any official chat or community surface, and anywhere someone is representing the project in public.

## Enforcement

Concerns can be raised by opening a private security advisory on the repo or by emailing the current maintainer set (listed in `MAINTAINERS.md` once that file lands). Reports are read by a human; we'll respond within seven days with a clear next step. Confidentiality is respected.

Consequences scale: a private nudge, a public warning, a temporary ban from project spaces, a permanent ban. Maintainers may act unilaterally to remove submissions, comments, or commits that violate this code; appeals go to the broader maintainer set.
